About

Care that arrives when families need it.

Aldea, in Spanish, means “village.” We’re building one for Florida families navigating autism, ADHD, learning differences and developmental care.

Most Florida families with a child who needs an autism or ADHD evaluation are told to wait six to nine months. By the time they’re seen, the school year is over, the IEP is delayed and the family has spent thousands of unreimbursed dollars trying to fill the gap.

Aldea Connect changes that by curating a network of clinicians who have actual openings — and pairing each family with a care navigator who knows what to ask, how to verify benefits, and how to make sure care actually begins.

Our principles

  • Families first. Free for parents, always. We’re paid by providers, not by the visit.
  • Real openings only. If a clinician doesn’t have capacity, they’re not on the platform that month.
  • Neurodiversity-affirming. Every provider is trained to support — not “fix” — neurodivergent children.
  • Florida-focused. We know the local school districts, Medicaid waivers and ABA carriers, because we live here too.
